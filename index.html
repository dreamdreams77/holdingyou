Holding You

Production & Arrangement Notes

Written and Produced by Laura Johnston

Overall Vision

Mood: Intimate but burdened. Heavy pulse beneath breathy truth. Emotional gravity without losing closeness. Survival, not sweetness.

Tempo & Key

BPM: 74–80 (slow but driving)

Key: D minor or C minor (darker emotional tone)

Chord Progressions

Verse: Dm – Bb – F – Gm

Chorus: Bb – Dm – Gm – F

Bridge: Gm – Dm – Bb – C

Final Chorus: Bb – Dm – Gm – Dm

Vocal Production

Lead Vocal Style: Breathy with more chest resonance underneath. Occasional strained or cracked edges on emotional lines. Double-tracked low vocal in choruses for weight.

Breath Placement:

  • Verses: Audible but heavier, like someone steadying themselves
  • Before "Holding, just holding": Deeper inhale, almost trembling
  • Bridge: Breath becomes part of the rhythm — intentional, slow
  • Final chorus: Controlled but weighted, less airy, more grounded

Instrumentation

Pads

Analog synths with slight distortion (Juno + Prophet blend). Add grit, widen with chorus effect.

Bass

Thick, warm sub with gentle overdrive. Saturated sub, sidechain subtly to kick.

Percussion

Deep kick, muted toms, metallic shimmers, reverse impacts.

Textures

Granular noise beds, reversed vocal fragments, low growl synths.

Arrangement

Intro: Sub-bass rumble + slow, cinematic pulse.

Verse: Dark pads, low-passed plucks, heartbeat kick with reverb tail.

Chorus: Heavy bass swell, wide stereo pads, distorted texture layers.

Bridge: Drop to a single drone with tension-building risers.

Final Chorus: Add gritty low-end, subtle saturation, emotional lift without going "big."

Mix Specifications

Reverb: Dark plate, long tail on textures, short on vocals.

Stereo: Verses narrow and tense, choruses wide and heavy.

Dynamics: Verses low/tense/simmering. Chorus emotional heaviness. Bridge minimal/ominous. Final chorus full weight/grounded/survival-tone.
