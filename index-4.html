Holding You

Production & Arrangement Notes

Written and Produced by Laura Johnston

BPM: 74–80 (slow but driving)  |  Key: D minor or C minor

Mood: Intimate but burdened. Heavy pulse beneath breathy truth. Survival, not sweetness.

Chord Progressions

Verse: Dm – Bb – F – Gm
Chorus: Bb – Dm – Gm – F
Bridge: Gm – Dm – Bb – C
Final Chorus: Bb – Dm – Gm – Dm

Vocal Production

Lead vocal breathy with chest resonance underneath. Occasional strained or cracked edges on emotional lines. Double-tracked low vocal in choruses for weight. Whisper harmonies replaced with shadowy, distorted backing layers.

Breath Placement: Verses audible but heavier, like someone steadying themselves. Before "Holding, just holding" — deeper inhale, almost trembling. Bridge breath becomes part of the rhythm. Final Chorus controlled but weighted, less airy, more grounded.

Mix: Slight saturation, close and centered, boost low mids for warmth.

Instrumentation

PadsAnalog synths with slight distortion (Juno + Prophet blend). Grit, widened with chorus effect.
BassThick warm sub with gentle overdrive. Saturated, sidechained subtly to kick.
PercussionDeep kick, muted toms, metallic shimmers, reverse impacts.
TexturesGranular noise beds, reversed vocal fragments, low growl synths, cinematic booms, filtered noise sweeps.

Arrangement

Intro: Sub-bass rumble + slow cinematic pulse.

Verse: Dark pads, low-passed plucks, heartbeat kick with reverb tail. Narrow and tense.

Chorus: Heavy bass swell, wide stereo pads, distorted texture layers. Emotional heaviness, not brightness.

Bridge: Drop to single drone with tension-building risers. Minimal but ominous.

Final Chorus: Gritty low-end, subtle saturation, emotional lift without going "big." Full weight, grounded, survival-tone.

Spatial Design

Reverb: Dark plate, long tail on textures, short on vocals.

Stereo: Verses narrow and tense. Choruses wide and heavy.
